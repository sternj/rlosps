Plan for instruction

What is the teacher doing?
What are the students doing?
Checks for Understanding
Teacher: Today you will be moving around the classroom to follow an algorithm. An algorithm is a sequence of events. Many of you have used Scratch block-based coding in the past. Today,we will use this to move around the room
Students will listen as the teacher reviews block-based coding and an algorithm.
Teacher will check that students are following the posted algorithms in the correct sequence.
Teacher will put an unplugged code on the board for students to follow. This can be printed out and hung up or put under the projector using the smartboard
Teacher will stop and give feedback if they do not
Teacher: Remember that in block-based coding, the algorithm is followed in order from top to bottom
Students will stand up behind their desks and make sure they have room to move around. Students will follow the first algorithm with the teacher.
Teacher actsout the algorithm posted with the students two times
Teacher posts another algorithm and has students act it out on their own and watches how they follow the algorithm RLM
Students will follow the second algorithm on their own
Teacher: Turn and talk about the answers to these questions
Students will turn and talk about the questions asked and then share out their thinking
What was difficult about this task? Were you able to follow the algorithm exactly? Do you think you can make your own algorithm?
What is the teacher doing?
What are the students doing?
Checks for Understanding
Important to thoughtfully pair students before lesson (SPED with regular ed, Higher EL with lower EL of same language, keep behaviors in mind etc.)
Students are working in pairs to create their own 4 -event algorithm. They will cut and glue it onto a piece of paper. RLM
Teachers will be circulating and checking sequencing.
Explain to students that they will now be constructing their own algorithms for another partner group to follow. Student should be placed in pairs for this activity so they can work together. They should also be assigned another partnership to work with once the algorithms are complete. RLM
Partners will get with another pair and switch algorithms. The partners will have to follow the algorithm of the other group.
Asking probing questions, “Are you sure that goes before this?” “Will other students know what you mean by this language?” etc
Teacher: Today you will be making your own algorithm to see if another group of students can follow. Your algorithm will have 4 events just like the ones you just followed with me
Students will make sure the other group is following their algorithm correctly. RLM
You will be able to use blocks that are already made and cut up to put your algorithm together, or you can use the blank template to make up your own algorithm.
You will need to put your algorithm in the exact order you want your classmates to complete the steps in, from top to bottom
What is the teacher doing?
What are the students doing?
Checks for Understanding
Teacher asks: What did you learn about making an algorithm?
Turn and talk in their groups about these questions and then report out to the class.
Teacher walks around and listens to conversations.
What did you have to know to make sure the other group followed your algorithm correctly?
Allow time for students to fill in any new vocabulary in their Computer Science Vocabulary Graphic Organizer. RLM
Teacher will take notes on which students felt this was difficult and who did not for future partnerships.
Do you think you will be able to make an algorithm like this on the computer
Have students discuss questions with the students in their groups and then have volunteers share out. RLM
