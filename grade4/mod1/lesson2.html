Plan for instruction

What is the teacher doing?
What are the students doing?
Checks for Understanding
Teacher will model breaking apart a simple multiplicative comparison word probing using the 3-reads orgaizer based on Ready Math.
Tracking teacher.
Can students find the important infromation in the word problem?
Ruby has 3 cats. Jane has 2 times as many cats as Ruby. How many cats does Jane have?
Participating in conversation about the word problem and helping teacher fill in organizer and solve.
Can students make an equation to match the word problem?
First Read: Write a movie title for the problem. Involve the students and have them share out titles they came up with for the problem.
T&T with sentence starters- What are we trying to find out?
Can students figure out whether to multiply or divide to solve?
Second Read: Fill in what we are trying to find and write a target sentence answering the question. (How many cats does Jane have? Jane has ___ cats.)
What is the important information in the problem?
Third Read: Fill in important information from the problem to help us solve (Ruby has 3 cats. Jane has 2 times as many cast as Ruby.)
What equation will match the word problem?
Have students help throughout this process. T&T about the problem and share out to help fill in the organizer.
Are we going to multiply or divide to solve?
Focus on visualizing the problem. Draw Ruby with her 3 cats. Think out loud: If Jane has 2 times as many cats as Ruby, who has more cookies? Jane. Let's write an equation that goes with this problem: 2 X R = J Should we multiply or divide to solve? Draw Jane and represent 2 times as many by drawing 2 circles for the groups. Draw 3 cats in each group. Count how many cats Jane has and solve by multiplying 2 X 3 = 6. Fill in the target sentence we made in the beginning. Jane has 6 cats. RLM
Remind students that they will be creating their visual on Scratch in the following lesson.
What is the teacher doing?
What are the students doing?
Checks for Understanding
Give each pair of students one word problem from the Fluency and Skills practice pages to solve and visualize.
Students will get their own word problem to solve using the organizer the teacher used previously to model the word problem.
Can students find the important information in the word problem?
Present students with Scratch Project checklist. They will be able to see what will be expected of them to complete the project. Check off today’s tasks and look ahead.
Once solved, students will draw out a visual of their word problem on blank white paper.
Can students make an equation to match the word problem?
Walking around monitoring student conversation and work
Students will have criteria for success (posted on anchor chart) to make sure they complete all steps
Can students figure out whether to multiply or divide to solve?
Asking guiding questions.
1. Read 3 times and fill in what you need to find, what is important, and a target sentence.
Are students drawing visuals that represent the problem correctly?
Talk with groups about their plan to visualize the problem. Pull small groups of students if they are having trouble. RLM
2. Write an equation to help you solve using a variable.
Are students working together collaboratively? (or is one student doing all the work?)
3. Decide whether you will multiply or divide. 4. Draw a visual to represent your problem that you will recreate on Scratchbe creative! 5. Solve equation and fill in target sentence with an answer
What is the teacher doing?
What are the students doing?
Checks for Understanding
Refer to essential question: How does making a visual help us solve a multiplicative comparison word problem?
Did students use the visual to solve or was it an extra piece?
Have students share out their experiences with solving the problem and making a visual.
Share out ideas about how they solved the problem and if the visual helped them or not.
Was it difficult to draw a picture of the problem? If so, what made it difficult? (Possible answers: too many objects to draw – ex. 45 stars)
Share out struggles and successes in the process
Did your visual help you solve the problem? RLM
